# Quadradius Powers - Complete Reference

> Research Date: 2025-12-14
> Source: https://quadradius.ddns.net/directions.html

## Overview

Quadradius features **87 distinct powers** organized by effect type. Powers come in three targeting variants:
- **Radial**: Affects surrounding tiles/pieces (8 adjacent squares)
- **Row**: Affects entire horizontal row
- **Column**: Affects entire vertical column

All 87 powers are available to all players.

## Complete Power List

### Piece Generation

#### Multiply
> "Generates a new piece in the arena. This new piece will have no powers of its own, but by adding pieces, you gain more coverage and control of the battle arena and limit your opponent's movement."

**Placement**: Player chooses from available adjacent tiles.

**Use Case**: Expand board control, create backup pieces, block opponent movement.

#### Cancel Multiply
> "If you changed your mind about multiplying this piece, or realized you can't move this new piece because it is surrounded by off-limit tiles, you can cancel your choice and choose another strategy."

**Use Case**: Undo multiply action when placement is unfavorable.

---

### Arena Control

#### Orbic Rehash
> "Removes all the existing Power Orbs in the arena and replaces them with newly positioned ones. Useful when the current layout is benefiting your opponent, whereas a rehashed layout of orbs may benefit you more."

**Use Case**: Reset orb positions when opponent has better access to current orbs.

### Range Enhancement

#### Grow Quadradius
> "Extends the effective range of row, column, and radial type powers. Row and column powers are widened, while radial powers extend farther outward. Each time it's used, the Quadradius is extended even farther."

**Range Scaling**:
| Stacks | Radial Reach | Multiplier |
|--------|--------------|------------|
| 0 (base) | 8 tiles | 1x |
| 1x Grow | 24 tiles | 3x |
| 2x Grow | 48 tiles | 6x |
| 3x Grow | Nearly entire arena | Max |

**Maximum**: 3 stacks per piece.

**Powers Enhanced by Grow Quadradius**:
Plateau, Moat, Trenches, Walls, Inverts, Teaches, Learns, Pilfers, Parasites, Scrambles, Swaps, Spywares, Orb Spys, Purifys, Tripwires, Inhibits, Kamikazes, Destroys, Acidics, Recruits, Bombs, Smart Bombs, Snake Tunneling.

**Strategy**: Only valuable if the piece has or will acquire range-type powers (radial, row, column). Prioritize collecting range powers on a piece with Grow Quadradius.

**Use Case**: Stack up to 3x to extend power range across nearly the entire arena. Critical for long-range attacks and devastating combos.

### Explosive Powers

#### Bombs
> "Unleashes a round of bombs into the entire arena. They not only devastate any piece they impact but they also compress tiles down deeper, eventually leaving a hole in the arena floor."

**Use Case**: Mass destruction when you have fewer pieces than opponent. Note: Affects YOUR pieces too!

#### Smart Bombs
> "Unleashes a round of smart bombs that will avoid your own squadron. They not only devastate any piece they impact but they also compress tiles down deeper, eventually leaving a hole in the arena floor."

**Use Case**: Safe mass destruction - only hits opponents. Creates permanent terrain damage.

#### Snake Tunneling
> "Energy tunnels below your piece, blindly seeking out opponents to destroy. This energy burrows thru the arena, randomly exploring, backtracking, and criss-crossing, forcing upwards any tile it encounters."

**Use Case**: Random destruction that also raises terrain. Unpredictable but can hit multiple enemies.

### Terrain Manipulation

#### Raise Tile
> "Some tiles may be too high to step up to. By raising a tile high enough, you can climb out of holes or step onto higher platforms."

**Use Case**: Create stepping stones, escape pits, reach elevated platforms.

#### Lower Tile
> "You can lower a tile to form strategically placed stepping stones. If you have pieces trapped in a pit, a higher up piece can lower tiles to carve out steps."

**Use Case**: Create paths, trap opponents in pits, carve escape routes.

#### Climb Tile
> "Able to move to a tile no matter how high it is. Great for scaling walls, reaching platforms, or getting out of pits. Eliminates the need for steps."

**Use Case**: Bypass elevation restrictions entirely. Escape any terrain trap.

#### Plateau
> "Takes your tile and the surrounding tiles and raises them to their maximum elevation. Gives you the upper ground, protects you from approaching attackers and helps control more territory."

**Use Case**: Create defensive high ground, block approaches, dominate area.

#### Moat

**Use Case**: Defensive isolation, trap nearby opponents in lowered tiles.

#### Trench Row
> "Digs a horizontal trench across the arena. Can be used to trap pieces in a trench or to separate the top area from the bottom area and gain more control."

**Use Case**: Divide arena horizontally, trap pieces, create barriers.

#### Trench Column
> "Digs a vertical trench across the arena. Can be used to trap pieces in a trench or to separate the left area from the right area and gain more control."

**Use Case**: Divide arena vertically, trap pieces, create barriers.

#### Wall Row
> "Builds a horizontal wall across the arena. Can be used to reach higher platforms or to separate the top area from the bottom area and gain more control."

**Use Case**: Create elevated path, divide arena, block movement.

#### Wall Column
> "Builds a vertical wall across the arena. Can be used to reach higher platforms or to separate the left area from the right area and gain more control."

**Use Case**: Create elevated path, divide arena, block movement.

#### Invert Radial
> "All the surrounding tiles will have their elevations inverted. Raised tiles will become lowered, and vice versa. Can be used to defend against opponents by upsetting the arena landscape."

**Use Case**: Disrupt terrain, flip high ground to low, create chaos.

#### Invert Row
> "All tiles in the row will have their elevations inverted. Raised tiles will become lowered, and vice versa. Can be used to defend against opponents by upsetting the arena landscape."

**Use Case**: Row-wide terrain disruption.

#### Invert Column
> "All tiles in the column will have their elevations inverted. Raised tiles will become lowered, and vice versa. Can be used to defend against opponents by upsetting the arena landscape."

**Use Case**: Column-wide terrain disruption.

#### Dredge Radial > "All of your surrounding pieces are raised to their max height while your opponent's are lowered to their deepest. Gives you the higher ground, while trapping and burying your opponent."

**Use Case**: Gain massive terrain advantage over nearby enemies.

#### Dredge Row > "All of the pieces in your row are raised to their max height while your opponent's are lowered to their deepest. Gives you the higher ground, while trapping and burying your opponent."

**Use Case**: Row-wide terrain domination.

#### Dredge Column > "All of the pieces in your column are raised to their max height while your opponent's are lowered to their deepest. Gives you the higher ground, while trapping and burying your opponent."

### Power Transfer

#### Teach Radial
> "A piece will teach the powers that it possesses to your surrounding pieces. Great way to quickly produce more powers. It will only work on your own pieces."

**Use Case**: Distribute powers to nearby allies. Critical for power multiplication strategy.

**Related**: Opposite of [Learn](#learn-radial) (give vs receive). Countered by [Inhibit](#inhibit-radial).

#### Teach Row
> "A piece will teach the powers that it possesses to your pieces in the same row. Great way to quickly produce more powers. It will only work on your own pieces."

**Use Case**: Row-wide power distribution.

#### Teach Column
> "A piece will teach the powers that it possesses to your pieces in the same column. Great way to quickly produce more powers. It will only work on your own pieces."

**Use Case**: Column-wide power distribution.

#### Learn Radial
> "A piece will learn all the powers of your surrounding pieces. Great way to obtain more powers and create a highly skilled piece. However, you can not learn from any opponents."

**Use Case**: Concentrate powers onto one piece from nearby allies.

**Related**: Opposite of [Teach](#teach-radial) (receive vs give). See [Pilfer](#pilfer-radial) to steal from enemies.

#### Learn Row
> "A piece will learn all the powers of your pieces in the same row. Great way to obtain more powers and create a highly skilled piece. However, you can not learn from any opponents."

**Use Case**: Row-wide power absorption.

#### Learn Column
> "A piece will learn all the powers of your pieces in the same column. Great way to obtain more powers and create a highly skilled piece. However, you can not learn from any opponents."

**Use Case**: Column-wide power absorption.

#### Pilfer Radial
> "A piece will steal all the powers from your opponents that are surrounding you. Great way to quickly obtain more powers and create a highly skilled piece, while leaving your opponent powerless."

**Use Case**: Steal enemy powers - double benefit of gaining and denying.

**Related**: One-time theft vs [Parasite](#parasite-radial) ongoing leech. See [Learn](#learn-radial) for ally version.

#### Pilfer Row
> "A piece will steal all the powers from your opponents that are in the same row. Great way to quickly obtain more powers and create a highly skilled piece, while leaving your opponent powerless."

**Use Case**: Row-wide power theft.

#### Pilfer Column
> "A piece will steal all the powers from your opponents that are in the same column. Great way to quickly obtain more powers and create a highly skilled piece, while leaving your opponent powerless."

**Use Case**: Column-wide power theft.

#### Parasite Radial
> "Leeches onto the power absorbing mechanism of opponents surrounding you. Any forthcoming powers they acquire, so do you. Also shares leeched powers with your whole network."

**Use Case**: Ongoing power duplication from enemies. Network-wide benefit.

**Related**: Ongoing leech vs [Pilfer](#pilfer-radial) one-time theft. Countered by [Purify](#purify-radial).

#### Parasite Row
> "Leeches onto the power absorbing mechanism of opponents in your row. Any forthcoming powers they acquire, so do you. Also shares leeched powers with your whole network."

**Use Case**: Row-wide power leeching.

#### Parasite Column
> "Leeches onto the power absorbing mechanism of opponents in your column. Any forthcoming powers they acquire, so do you. Also shares leeched powers with your whole network."

**Use Case**: Column-wide power leeching.

### Power Multiplication

#### 2x
> "Doubles the amount of powers your piece currently possesses. Best strategy is to wait until you acquire as many powers as possible before using it."

**Use Case**: Wait until piece has many powers, then double. Exponential growth potential.

#### Beneficiary
> "All the other pieces in your squadron sacrifice their powers to this one piece, possibly creating a super piece with an impressive inventory. This piece will have many strategic, combinational attacks."

**Use Case**: Create a super-powered piece. Risk: all eggs in one basket.

### Movement Powers

#### Move Again
> "It's still your turn after moving the piece. Great for moving a piece into position and following up with an attack. Using 2 or more of these allows for multiple moves in a single round."

**Use Case**: Extra actions. Stack for multiple moves per turn. Critical for combos.

**Related**: Essential for power combos. Countered by [Tripwire](#tripwire-radial) (each move triggers).

#### Move Diagonal
> "This piece can now also move on a diagonal. Can be used as a way to move more quickly, slip between diagonal walls or over diagonal pits, or can be used to take an opponent by surprise."

**Use Case**: 8-directional movement, bypass terrain, surprise attacks.

#### Flat To Sphere
> "This piece can now move from one side of the arena to the other. It treats the playing field as if the flat arena is now a sphere with no physical edges."

**Use Case**: Wraparound movement - escape corners, surprise from edges.

#### Relocate
> "This piece will be randomly moved to a new, unoccupied area in the arena. Useful when your position could not be worsened, such as being backed against the wall, or having no Power Orbs close."

**Use Case**: Emergency escape, random repositioning when trapped.

#### Hotspot
> "Changes an ordinary tile into a hotspot where any piece in your squadron can move to, regardless of how far away it is or its elevation. You can even jump on Power Orbs that appear or opponents."

**Use Case**: Create teleport destination. Strategic positioning for entire team.

#### Scavenger > "Your piece will be equipped to scavenge through a defeated opponent after jumping on it, sharing its powers among your network. If activated a second time, you can jump onto your own squadron."

**Use Case**: Inherit powers from destroyed enemies. Second activation: sacrifice allies for powers.

#### Power Plant > "Plants a power distribution device on the tile below the piece. Any Power Orb that spawns on this distribution block will be automatically absorbed by all pieces in the aligned Power Plant grid."

**Use Case**: Automatic power distribution network.

#### Network Bridge > "This piece will tap into all the networked powers activated by others in your squadron. Any power that is normally absorbed through the squadron-wide network, is now absorbed by this piece."

**Use Case**: Concentrate networked powers onto one piece.

### Positioning Powers

**Use Case**: Instant position swap. Second activation: swap with enemies.

#### Centerpult > "Your piece can catapult itself into the vacant center of any four, symmetric pieces, as long as occupying the empty, center tile will complete either a plus sign or an 'x' shaped pattern."

**Use Case**: Tactical positioning into formation centers.

## Power Interaction Notes

### Terminology

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Squadron** | All of your pieces |
| **Network** | Pieces connected by a shared power (e.g., all pieces with Parasite form a network and share leeched powers among themselves) |

### Overheat Rule
> "If you get 10 or more of any single power on any single piece, you can overheat."

**Consequence: The piece explodes and is destroyed.**

Avoid stacking 10+ of the same power on one piece.

### Power Stacking

- **Grow Quadradius**: Stacks up to 3x for maximum range
- **Move Again**: Stacks for multiple turns
- **2x**: Doubles ALL current powers (use after collecting many)

### Defensive Counters

| Attack | Counter |
|--------|---------|
| Jump | Jump Proof |
| Power theft | Inhibit |
| Debuffs | Purify |
| Terrain traps | Climb Tile |
| Position attacks | Scramble, Relocate |
